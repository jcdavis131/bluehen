#### 2\. Biological Foundations: Synaptic Homeostasis and Localized Downscaling

The integration of biological neuro-analogues provides a blueprint for efficient network recovery. ASN is grounded in the  **Synaptic Homeostasis Hypothesis (SHY)** , which identifies Non-Rapid Eye Movement (NREM) sleep as a localized, homeostatic regulator. In mammalian systems, sleep is the metabolic price paid for the plasticity acquired during wakefulness; it serves to down-select synapses, thereby restoring the effective rank of neural ensembles.**Localized Synaptic Downscaling**  operationalizes this by treating transformer blocks as independent modules. Just as optogenetic studies show that cortical "ON/OFF" periods can fulfill sleep functions locally without global quiescence, ASN monitors the "metabolic cost" (the  $L\_2$  norm and ER) of specific layers. When a layer becomes oversaturated with dataset-specific bias, the system triggers a localized rest cycle.This mechanism is driven by  **Heterosynaptic Plasticity** . Unlike Hebbian (homosynaptic) updates that merely reinforce active pathways, heterosynaptic logic provides a counter-balance. It aggressively prunes weak, noisy dendritic connections—the artificial equivalent of spurious correlations—while actively protecting the receptor density of consolidated memory traces. By mathematically operationalizing these biological rhythms, we can perform surgical interventions on the model's spectral health.

By minimizing  $H(Z\_2)$ , we mathematically guarantee an increase in the semantic utility of the core encoder, ensuring it remains isotropic and untainted by the geometric saturation of the contrastive loss.
