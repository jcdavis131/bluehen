## Introduction to Hard Negatives
Hard negatives are essential for training effective retrieval models [arxiv:2511.08029]. They are typically obtained through hard-negative mining, which relies on ranking documents using cross-encoders or static embedding models based on similarity metrics such as cosine distance [arxiv:2511.08029]. However, the process of hard-negative mining can be challenging, especially in domains like biomedicine and science, where distinguishing between source and hard negative documents can be difficult [arxiv:2511.08029].

## Characteristics of Hard Negatives
Hard negatives are documents that are similar to the source document but not duplicates [arxiv:2511.08029]. They can be used to improve the performance of retrieval models by providing a more nuanced understanding of the relationships between documents [arxiv:2506.02020]. The effective mining of hard negative samples is critical for enhancing the performance of contrastive learning paradigms [arxiv:2506.02020].

## Methods for Hard Negative Mining
Several methods have been proposed for hard negative mining, including offline and online strategies [arxiv:2506.02020]. These approaches aim to identify hard negative samples that can be used to improve the performance of retrieval models [arxiv:2506.02020]. Some methods also incorporate additional techniques, such as self-paced modulation terms, to downweight the loss associated with easy negatives and encourage the model to focus on hard negatives [arxiv:2310.06918].

## Applications of Hard Negatives
Hard negatives have been applied in various domains, including biomedicine [arxiv:2511.08029], enterprise search [arxiv:2505.18366], and multilingual semantic search [arxiv:2603.06586]. They have been shown to improve the performance of retrieval models in these domains, leading to substantial gains in recall and other evaluation metrics [arxiv:2505.18366, arxiv:2603.06586].
